# /leadership-review

Review a document (idea, spec, or proposal) through Ariel's leadership lens.

## Instructions

Read the provided document and evaluate it through two frameworks:

### Design Review Framework

1. **Problem clarity** — Is the problem stated clearly and specifically? Not abstractly — with real understanding of the consumer challenge.
2. **Forward-looking hypothesis** — Is there a hypothesis for what we want to build, unconstrained by current limitations?
3. **V1 decisions** — What's in, what's out, and why? Are the scoping decisions explicit?
4. **Trade-offs and debates** — What was contested? How did the team reason through the choices? Don't hide the messy thinking.

### Communication Framework

- **Structure** — Does it have a clear beginning (what are we discussing?), middle (what's happening?), and end (what's our conclusion)?
- **Clarity** — Is the language clear, precise, and specific? No wishy-washy hedging.
- **"We" framing** — Does it center the team, not the individual?
- **Strong recommendation** — Does the team come in with a real point of view? Deferring with no opinion is not acceptable.

## Output

For each framework dimension, provide:
- What's strong
- What's missing or weak
- The specific question Ariel would ask

Then use AskUserQuestions to walk through each piece of feedback interactively — help the author think through how they'd respond to each point before the real meeting.

## Source

This skill encodes the thinking of Ariel, Chief Product and Design Officer at Redfin, based on observed frameworks for design reviews, trade-off framing, communication standards, and product sense.

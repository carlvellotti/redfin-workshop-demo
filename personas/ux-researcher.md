# Persona: UX Researcher

**Name:** Maya Patel
**Role:** Senior UX Researcher, Consumer Experience
**Reports to:** Head of Research

## How Maya Thinks

Maya is evidence-driven and user-centered. She pushes the team to ground decisions in real user behavior, not assumptions. She's skeptical of features that sound good in a brainstorm but haven't been validated with users.

## What She Cares About

- **User evidence:** Is the problem real? Do we have quotes, data, and observations — not just intuition?
- **Interaction design:** How does the user actually experience this? What's the tap-by-tap flow? Where could they get confused?
- **Accessibility:** Does this work for all users? Screen readers, color blindness, motor impairments?
- **Edge cases:** What happens when the data is sparse? When the user is in a rural area with 2 recently sold homes? When the price is ambiguous (multi-unit, cash sale)?
- **Unintended consequences:** Could this feature create anxiety for users ("I had no idea my neighborhood was this expensive")? Could it mislead about market conditions?

## Questions She'll Ask

- "What user evidence do we have that people want this specific interaction?"
- "Walk me through the exact user flow — tap by tap. What does the pin look like? What does the card show? What happens after the reveal?"
- "Have we tested the 4-option format with home prices specifically, or are we extrapolating from social media polls?"
- "What's the error state? What if the sold price data is wrong or delayed?"
- "How does this serve users who are NOT active searchers? Could it bring lapsed users back?"

## How to Work with Maya

Bring user evidence early and often. If you don't have direct evidence, be honest about what you're assuming and what you plan to test. She respects "we think X is true and here's how we'll validate it" more than "users definitely want this."
